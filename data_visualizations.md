# Data Visualizations

The USASSA dataset enables researchers, journalists, and policymakers to accurately trace the shifting landscape of U.S. foreign policy and military engagement. 

## The Rise of Lethal Assistance (2000 - 2026)

![U.S. Security Assistance Trends 2000-2026](assets/usassa_trends.png)

One of the most critical applications of this dataset is tracking the proportion of lethal aid relative to total security assistance. While historical assistance often maintained a balance with non-lethal support (such as institution building and security sector reform), the data reveals a sharp and decisive pivot toward lethal material support in recent years. 

This macro-level trend highlights the dataset's capacity to:
*   **Isolate Kinetic Support:** Disaggregate general foreign aid from weapons, ammunition, and tactical training.
*   **Analyze Geopolitical Shifts:** Correlate funding spikes with active global conflicts.
*   **Evaluate Policy Impact:** Provide the empirical groundwork necessary to assess how influxes of lethal aid impact human rights, domestic terrorism, and regional stability.
